Smoked CheesePuffs

I bought my husband a Yoder smoker last year, and boy that thing is a beast! We still have our Traeger, but he wanted something bigger. Bigger is better, right?

I remember trying this recipe for the first time and beign super leary of what they would taste like. Boy was I suprised when they were done! I haven't yet, but may try smoking some potato chips some day.

If you don't have a smoker, you could use a smoke tube like the one we use to cold smoke cheese, LIZZA Premium Pellet Smoker Tube The tube gets stuffed with pellets, lit and smokes for hours. You could even use the smoke tube to add smokey flavor to other dishes as well

To smoke cheese puffs you will use a low temp on your smoker, or use a smoke tube and cold smoke them. You do NOT want to use high heat. Let's get smoking!

Course Appetizer
Cuisine American BBQ
Prep Time 2
Cook Time 15 minutes
Servings Depends

Materials

  • Smoker or smoke tube
  • Aluminum foil pain
  • Pellets, any flavor

Ingredients

  • 1 tub of cheese puffs
  • Cooking spray
  • Butt rub (or any other preferred seasoning)

Instructions

  1. Turn smoker on and set to Smoke setting
  2. Place cheese puffs in an aluminum pan
  3. Lightly spray cheese puffs with cooking spray
  4. Sprinkle butt rub, or other seasoning, on cheese puffs being careful not to use too mcuh
  5. Place foil pan on smoker, close lid
  6. Smoke for 15 minutes, shaking pan half way through
  7. Pour cheese puffs in ziploc bag and seal
  8. Keep cheese puffs sealed for at least 2 hours
